Preston and surrounding counties dodged a real serious winter weather disaster. Unlike much of the North East, West Virginia was hit with a smaller storm which dumped anywhere from four to potentially six inches of snow and causing a handful of accidents and school delays. According to the Preston County Office of Emergency Services, a few dozen vehicle wrecks and other weather related incidents were reported. Public Information Officer Clark Nicklow says the worst was actually on Saturday due to severe icy conditions on the roadways. “The ice caused a rash of accidents,” Nicklow said. “Everything king of happened around after dark but things starting clearing up pretty fast later in the evening.”
